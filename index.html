Classic Models (Pre-1990s)

Mercedes-Benz 300SL (1954-1963)

  • Engine: 3.0L Inline-6 engine (M198), Direct fuel injection, Overhead camshaft, Dry sump lubrication
  • Performance: 215 hp, 203 lb-ft, Top Speed: 160 mph, 0-60 mph: 7.4 seconds
  • Transmission: 4-speed manual
  • Dimensions: Length: 4520 mm, Width: 1790 mm, Height: 1300 mm, Wheelbase: 2400 mm, Curb weight: 1295 kg
  • Notable Features: Gullwing doors, Lightweight tubular frame, Aluminum body panels, Independent suspension, Hydraulic drum brakes
  • Historical Significance: Regarded as the first "supercar", Significant race victories including the 24 Hours of Le Mans

Mercedes-Benz 190 SL (1955-1963)

  • Engine: 1.9L Inline-4 engine (M121), Single overhead camshaft, Dual Solex carburetors
  • Performance: 120 hp, 105 lb-ft, Top Speed: 107 mph, 0-60 mph: 13 seconds
  • Transmission: 4-speed manual
  • Dimensions: Length: 4290 mm, Width: 1740 mm, Height: 1320 mm, Wheelbase: 2400 mm, Curb weight: 1140 kg
  • Notable Features: Convertible roadster design, Luxurious interior with leather seats, Optional hardtop, Fully synchronized gearbox
  • Historical Significance: Positioned as a more affordable, stylish roadster compared to the 300SL

1990s Models

Mercedes-Benz S-Class (W140, 1991-1998)

  • Engine Options: 3.2L Inline-6 (M104), 4.2L V8 (M119), 5.0L V8 (M119), 6.0L V12 (M120)
  • Performance: 228 hp to 402 hp, 229 lb-ft to 428 lb-ft, Top Speed: 155 mph, 0-60 mph: 6.6 seconds
  • Transmission: 4-speed automatic (early models), 5-speed automatic (later models)
  • Dimensions: Length: 5113-5179 mm, Width: 1886 mm, Height: 1486 mm, Wheelbase: 3040 mm, Curb weight: 2020-2200 kg
  • Notable Features: Double-pane window glazing, Electrically operated rearview mirrors, Soft-close doors and trunk, Optional V12 engine, Advanced safety features including airbags and ABS
  • Historical Significance: Represented Mercedes-Benz's commitment to luxury and technology, Known for its solidity and refinement

Mercedes-Benz E-Class (W210, 1995-2002)

  • Engine Options: 2.0L Inline-4 (M111), 2.8L Inline-6 (M104), 3.2L V6 (M112), 4.3L V8 (M113)
  • Performance: 134 hp to 302 hp, 140 lb-ft to 295 lb-ft, Top Speed: 149 mph, 0-60 mph: 6.1 seconds
  • Transmission: 5-speed automatic
  • Dimensions: Length: 4818 mm, Width: 1799 mm, Height: 1429 mm, Wheelbase: 2833 mm, Curb weight: 1545-1725 kg
  • Notable Features: Distinctive twin-headlamp design, Optional 4MATIC all-wheel-drive, Advanced electronic stability program (ESP), Luxurious interior with high-quality materials
  • Historical Significance: Continued the E-Class legacy as a versatile and reliable luxury sedan

Modern Models (2000s to Present)

Mercedes-Benz S-Class (W222, 2013-2020)

  • Engine Options: 3.0L V6 (S400 Hybrid), 4.7L V8 (S500), 6.0L V12 (S600), 4.0L V8 (AMG S63), 6.0L V12 (AMG S65)
  • Performance: 329 hp to 621 hp, 354 lb-ft to 738 lb-ft, Top Speed: 155 mph, 0-60 mph: 3.9 seconds
  • Transmission: 7-speed automatic (early models), 9-speed automatic (later models)
  • Dimensions: Length: 5116-5453 mm, Width: 1899 mm, Height: 1496 mm, Wheelbase: 3035-3365 mm, Curb weight: 2070-2380 kg
  • Notable Features: High-definition digital instrument cluster, Burmester 3D surround sound system, Energizing Comfort Control, MAGIC BODY CONTROL, Extensive driver assistance systems
  • Historical Significance: Represented the pinnacle of Mercedes-Benz luxury and technology

Mercedes-Benz E-Class (W213, 2016-Present)

  • Engine Options: 2.0L Inline-4 (E200), 3.0L Inline-6 (E450), 4.0L V8 (AMG E63)
  • Performance: 184 hp to 603 hp, 221 lb-ft to 627 lb-ft, Top Speed: 155 mph (limited), 186 mph (AMG E63 S), 0-60 mph: 3.3 seconds
  • Transmission: 9-speed automatic
  • Dimensions: Length: 4923 mm, Width: 1852 mm, Height: 1468 mm, Wheelbase: 2939 mm, Curb weight: 1680-1895 kg
  • Notable Features: Multibeam LED headlights, Dual 12.3-inch widescreen displays, MBUX with voice control, Active Distance Assist DISTRONIC, E-ACTIVE BODY CONTROL suspension
  • Historical Significance: Known for its innovation in infotainment and driver assistance systems
